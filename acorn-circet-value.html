What it is

A governed capture desk — not a lead list

ACORN scopes public opportunities against your capability profile, gathers the supporting research, and assembles a structured packet your team can act on in under 30 seconds. Two clean lanes, deliberately separate:

Lane A

ACORN — the capture desk

A 24/7 agent on the SAM.gov side: scopes opportunities against a Circet capability profile on the NAICS/PSC axes you specify, pulls the supporting research, and prepares a decision-ready review packet.

Lane B

SiteVision — field closeout

The field side: mobile site capture, photo & asset tagging, redlining → approval workflow → a finished closeout PDF. Aimed straight at the closeout overhead and jobsite waste your team named.

What it never does: claim eligibility, award probability, pricing, or authority to submit. It is not win-prediction and not auto-submission — and we are not claiming FedRAMP / SOC 2 / CUI today. Those stay honest, separate conversations.

Why it's credible

The moat is the evidence envelope

Public SAM.gov data is commodity — anyone can search it. The differentiator is how each opportunity is packaged: a fixed review contract where every fact is traceable and every gap is visible.

Contract

A fixed 10-part packet

Every packet answers the same six decision questions and renders the same sections — decision card, fit, scope, capture path, required documents, gates, schedule, risk register, checklist, evidence. A non-technical reviewer finds the priorities and next actions in under 30 seconds.

Provenance

A graded evidence ledger

Each lead records its source URLs, whether the notice was fetched, whether attachments were fetched and parsed, and a receipt id — so you see exactly how deep verification went before trusting a word.

Guardrail

A fail-closed floor

The generator is tested to be unable to claim eligibility, pricing, submission authority, or award probability — or any attachment fact it didn't actually review. It cannot fake confidence.

Posture

Public-source, secret-clean

SAM.gov is the wired source; other lanes are honestly labelled "not yet integrated." No customer credentials, no committed keys — enforced, not aspirational.
