Why it compounds

Every new station makes the last one stronger.

Because every station speaks the same language — receipts, lineage, policy — they compose. Evidence from the field (DORIS) can inform a capture decision (ACORN) and settle in commerce (ACE), and the whole chain replays as one story. One runtime, many businesses, a single account of record.

The pattern

Pick a domain where "who authorized this, and can you prove it?" is a real question — public procurement, field evidence, regulated commerce — and Thunderline turns it into a station with governance built in. That's the OKO model: not one product, a runtime that makes governed businesses cheap to build.
