## Pages changed

- `index.html`: rewrote the public home page message, navigation labels, metadata, core sections, CTA, and footer copy.

## Message change

### Before

The home page led with "governed AI operations" and quickly introduced runtime primitives, internal product names, industry capabilities, named integrations, territorial licensing, and technical implementation language. The central product was difficult to identify without already knowing the OKO and Thunderline vocabulary.

### After

The home page leads with one product model:

- A ThunderBlock is a managed hive and shared operating space.
- Agents are specialized workers in the hive.
- The human is the operator who sets intent, authority, and review points.
- Thunderline is the infrastructure layer connecting hives, memory, governance, communication, and execution.
- Prism is a window into the hive.
- ThunderPAC is an agent-facing cognition layer, not the whole product.

The page now explains the problem, the ThunderBlock, human-agent collaboration, shared memory and context, governance, use cases, and the engagement path in plain language.

## Stale or confusing claims removed

- Replaced a large named-integration catalog with capability-neutral governance and operating-evidence language.
- Reframed broad industry solution statements as use-case patterns for scoping.
- Removed broad sovereign, air-gapped, cloud-provider, multilingual, and territorial-licensing implications from the home-page pitch.
- Removed language suggesting specific station brands or vertical capabilities are universally deployed.
- Removed predictions about regulatory demand and enterprise standardization.
- Removed the dated internal-operations activation claim because it did not help explain the public product.

## Claims intentionally avoided

- No fully autonomous company or self-running business claim.
- No AGI or blockchain framing.
- No claim that every connector, deployment model, memory layer, receipt path, or agent capability is live.
- No claim that ACORN, TBX P4, PAC memory v0, or external secret/deployment work is shipped.
- No medical, legal, financial, compliance, or regulator-approval claim.
- No customer logos, customer counts, performance benchmarks, or deployment counts.
